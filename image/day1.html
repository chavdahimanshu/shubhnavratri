ABOUT

MAA SHAILAPUTRI

Shailaputri, the first form of Goddess Durga, is depicted as riding a bull and carrying a trident and a lotus in her hands. She is the embodiment of the power of Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva, the Holy Trinity. According to Hindu mythology, she is the reincarnation of Sati, the daughter of King Daksha and the wife of Lord Shiva.

The word "Shailaputri" is derived from two Sanskrit words: "Shaila" meaning mountain and "Putri" meaning daughter. She is called Shailaputri because she was born as the daughter of the king of the mountains, Himalaya. Devotees worship her on the first day of Navratri to seek her blessings for strength, courage, and determination.
