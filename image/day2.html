WELCOME TO SHUBH NAVRATRI DAY 2

ABOUT

Goddess Brahmacharini

Goddess Brahmacharini is a symbol of penance and devotion. She signifies the pursuit of knowledge and spiritual wisdom. Devotees worship her on the second day of Navratri to seek her blessings for peace, prosperity, and marital bliss.

During the second day of Navratri, devotees perform rituals, offer prayers, chant mantras, and observe fasts to honor Goddess Brahmacharini and seek her divine grace. Each day of Navratri holds significance and devotees celebrate the festival with great devotion and enthusiasm.

EVENTS

SINGER : KINJAL DAVE

Kinjal Dave is a popular Gujarati Female Singer. She is known for Gujarati Bhajans, Songs, Arti, Stage Performances, and Garba Nights. Popularly, She is also known as Garba Queen of Gujarat.

She is popular for her Stage Performances and Graba Nights. Kinjal Dave made her singing debut with the song – Char Char Bangadi Wali Gadi. Her first album released in 2015 – Jonadiyo. She has also got awards for her singing in Gujarat.

She has released more than 100 music album.

Traditational Cloths : Cloths color is Green

On the second day of Navratri, the color Green is traditionally worn. Green symbolizes growth, fertility, and auspiciousness. Many people choose to wear outfits predominantly in green, which can include sarees, salwar kameez, lehengas, or any traditional attire. It's common to see men and women, particularly those participating in dandiya and garba dances, dressed in vibrant green attire to celebrate the festival.

It's important to note that these color traditions might vary, and some regions or communities may follow different color sequences or have their own unique customs regarding clothing during Navratri.
