ABOUT

Goddess Chandraghanta

On the third day of Navratri, the Goddess Chandraghanta is worshipped. Chandraghanta is the third manifestation of Goddess Durga and is revered for her bravery and grace. Devotees worship her for peace, tranquility, and prosperity. Chandraghanta is depicted with ten arms, holding various weapons and symbols of power. She has a half-moon shaped like a bell on her forehead, giving her the name "Chandraghanta." The goddess rides a tiger, symbolizing strength and courage, and is known for her fearlessness in the face of challenges.

Worshipping Chandraghanta is believed to provide protection from evil forces and promote inner strength and spiritual growth. Devotees offer milk and sweets to please the goddess during the worship rituals.

Traditational Cloths : Cloths color is Yellow

Day 3 of Navratri is associated with the color Yellow. The color yellow represents brightness and energy. It symbolizes the color of the harvest and is often associated with positivity and knowledge.Many people choose to wear yellow-colored clothing on this day to celebrate and worship the goddess Durga or her various manifestations.

It's important to note that these color traditions might vary, and some regions or communities may follow different color sequences or have their own unique customs regarding clothing during Navratri.
