WELCOME TO SHUBH NAVRATRI DAY 4

ABOUT

Goddess Kushmanda

On the fourth day of Navratri, the worship is dedicated to Goddess Kushmanda. Kushmanda is considered the creator of the universe and is believed to have created the world with her divine smile. She is depicted as having eight arms and rides a lion. She holds various divine objects and rosary beads in her hands, symbolizing her divine powers.

They offer prayers, perform rituals, and chant mantras in her honor to invoke her divine energy and receive her blessings. Each day of Navratri is dedicated to a different form of Goddess Durga, and worshiping her during these nine days is considered highly auspicious in Hindu tradition.

EVENTS

SINGER : Kairavi Buch

Kairavi buch is a popular Gujarati Female Singer. she is known for Gujarati Bhajans, Songs, Arti, Stage Performances, and Garba Nights.

Kairavi with her exquisite beauty and melodious voice slays the internet every time and is a darling singer for this young generation. She has a wonderful ability to make an extraordinary fuse of Gujarati old classics with new songs. Thus her created music pieces always engage the young generation to have a better understanding of old classic and new modern songs.

This singing gem of Gujarat is making the world-shaking its legs with her tune. She has been performing for the Indian diaspora in Australia, New Zealand, Canada, UK, and the USA.

Traditational Cloths : Cloths color is Royal Blue

Day 4 of Navratri is associated with the color Royal Blue. Devotees often wear royal blue-colored clothing and decorations on this day to celebrate and honor the goddess. Goddess Kushmanda is considered the creator of the universe and is believed to bestow health, strength, and prosperity upon her devotees. Worshipping her on the fourth day of Navratri is a way to seek her blessings for a healthy and prosperous life.

It's important to note that these color traditions might vary, and some regions or communities may follow different color sequences or have their own unique customs regarding clothing during Navratri.
