WELCOME TO SHUBH NAVRATRI DAY 5

ABOUT

Goddess Skandamata

On the fifth day of Navratri, the worship is dedicated to Goddess Skandamata. Skandamata is the mother of Lord Kartikeya, also known as Skanda, the god of war. She is depicted holding her son Skanda in her lap and hence is known as Skandamata, the mother of Skanda. Skandamata is usually shown with four arms, riding a lion, and is a symbol of motherly love and protection.

Devotees worship Goddess Skandamata on the fifth day of Navratri to seek her blessings for courage, strength, and protection. It is believed that she blesses her devotees with fearlessness and the ability to overcome obstacles in life. Worshipping Skandamata during Navratri is a way of invoking the maternal energy of the divine and seeking her guidance and blessings.

SINGER : Geetaben Rabari

Geetaben Rabari is a popular Gujarati Female Singer. she is known for Gujarati Bhajans, Songs, Arti, Stage Performances, and Garba Nights.

Geetaben Rabari with her voice and melodies gradually gained popularity, and her name began to resonate throughout Gujarat. People began referring to Geeta Rabari as Kachhi Koyal because Geetaben Rabari was born in Kutch, Gujarat, and her name became Kachhi Koyal as a result.

she has released more than 20 music album.

Traditational Cloths : Cloths color is White

Day 5 of Navratri is White is a color associated with purity and peace, symbolizing divine grace and spiritual cleansing during Navratri. Devotees often wear white attire and decorate altars with white flowers to invoke a sense of serenity and seek the blessings of Goddess Durga for purity of heart and mind.

It's important to note that these color traditions might vary, and some regions or communities may follow different color sequences or have their own unique customs regarding clothing during Navratri.
