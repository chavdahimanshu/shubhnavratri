ABOUT

Goddess Kaalratri

  On the seventh day of Navratri, the Goddess Durga is worshiped in the form of Goddess Kaalratri. She is considered one of the most ferocious forms of Goddess Durga and is also known as Kali. In Sanskrit, 'Kaal' means time and 'Ratri' means night, so Kaalratri literally translates to the one who is the night of time or the dark night.

  Goddess Kaalratri is depicted with a dark complexion, disheveled hair, a fierce expression, and a necklace shining like thunder. She is usually shown riding a donkey and holding a sharp sword or a cleaver. In this form, she is believed to destroy all ignorance and bring light into the dark. Devotees seek her blessings to overcome fear and ignorance and to gain strength and courage.
