WELCOME TO SHUBH NAVRATRI DAY 9

ABOUT

GODDESS SIDDHIDATRI

On the ninth day of Navratri, Goddess Durga is worshipped in the form of Siddhidatri. She is believed to possess and bestow all types of Siddhis (spiritual or mystical powers) to her devotees. Siddhidatri is often depicted sitting on a lotus flower and is revered as the ultimate source of divine wisdom, granting her devotees knowledge and enlightenment.

Worshiping Goddess Siddhidatri on the ninth day of Navratri is believed to fulfill all the aspirations and desires of her devotees, bringing them spiritual fulfillment and divine blessings.

EVENTS

SINGER : KIRTIDAN GHADAVI

Kirtidan Gadhvi is a renowned folk and classical singer from Gujarat, India. He is particularly famous for his performances in the folk genre, including Bhavageet, Sugam Sangeet, and Dayro (a form of Gujarati folk music). Gadhvi is celebrated for his powerful and soulful voice, and he has a significant fan following, especially in Gujarat and among the Gujarati diaspora.

Gadhvi has contributed significantly to the promotion of Gujarati folk music and has performed at various cultural events and festivals both in India and abroad. He is known for his renditions of traditional Gujarati folk songs as well as devotional music.

TRADITIONAL CLOTH THEMES : RED

During Navratri, women often adorn themselves in striking red-colored attire, symbolizing the fiery energy of Goddess Durga. The vibrant red fabric elegantly drapes, capturing the essence of power and devotion, making it a popular choice, resonating with the festival's fervor and enthusiasm. This traditional red clothing not only pays homage to the goddess but also embodies the spirit of Navratri, creating a visually stunning and culturally significant celebration.
